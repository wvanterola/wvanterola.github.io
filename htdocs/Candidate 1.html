Maria Leonor "Leni" Gerona Robredo

Latest News

Supporters of presidential candidate and Vice President Maria Leonor "Leni" Robredo and her running mate, Sen. Francis "Kiko" Pangilinan, came out in droves on Saturday as they "painted the town" pink in support of their candidacies in the 2022 national elections.

The Tropa ng Pagasa: The National Proclamation Motorcade for #LeniKiko2022 was livestreamed on Facebook and YouTube, capturing the outpouring of support for Robredo and Pangilinan from at least 45 cities in the country.

Volunteer groups like "Lawyers for Leni," "Riders for Leni," "Bikers for Leni," the opposition coalition 1Sambayan, the People Power Volunteers for Reform (PPVR) and Leni Urban Poor were among those who joined the motorcade in Metro Manila that started at 10 a.m. and ended at noon.

Qualifications

Maria Leonor "Leni" Gerona Robredo is a Filipina lawyer, politician, and social activist who is the 14th and incumbent vice president of the Philippines. She served as the representative of Camarines Sur's 3rd district from 2013 until her inauguration as vice president in 2016. In the vice presidential election, Robredo defeated Bongbong Marcos.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, the Office of the Vice President (OVP) under Robredo provided free shuttle services for pandemic frontline workers, swab tests, telehealth services, and raised funds for relief operations across the country. Robredo is the second woman to serve as vice president of the Philippines after Gloria Macapagal Arroyo and the first vice president from Bicol Region. In 2016, Robredo was awarded by the Thai government for her work and advocacy in women empowerment and gender equality. Under her leadership, the OVP also received the ISO 9001: 2015 certification for the office's quality management systems.

On August 23, 2016, Robredo was awarded the Most Influential Filipina Woman of the World award by Filipina Women's Network (FWN), a non-government organization.

Three universities have conferred Robredo with honorary doctorates:

  • Polytechnic University of the Philippines (2015, Doctor in Public Administration)
  • University of Saint Anthony in Iriga, Camarines Sur, her home province (2017, Doctor of Humanities)
  • University of the Cordilleras (2017, Doctor of Laws)

Stands on various issues

While the Philippines may be considered one of the most gender equal countries in the world, Leni Robredo said much work still needed to be done to remove economic and social barriers that hinder Filipino women's empowerment.

Leni Robredo maintained her stance favoring same-sex unions in the Philippines but rejected the idea of decriminalizing abortion, saying local health services should instead be strengthened.

“I have always been very open about freedom of choice – ‘yung pagbigay ng kalayaan para sa lahat na i-pursue ‘yung gusto nila basta hindi ito makakasama sa kapwa niya,” Robredo said of same-sex civil unions.
